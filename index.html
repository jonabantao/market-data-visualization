Stock Market Over The Past Five Years

As of February 2018, the American stock market has been in a bull market for a consecutive nine years. While the S&P500 has risen approximately 89% in five years, the NASDAQ-100 has risen almost twice as high, having a total return of almost 156%.

This chart uses the D3.js library to show selected companies and their performance up to the past five years. All data is pulled from the IEX Developer API. Bubbles are sized according to their price-to-earnings ratio (P/E). For fundamental investors, a larger P/E generally means purchasing a share of a company is relatively expensive compared to one with a low P/E.

Note that there are some companies listed on the chart with a P/E ratio of "Not Applicable". Not Applicable P/E ratios are negative mainly from reporting a loss in earnings per share.
